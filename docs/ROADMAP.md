# Development Roadmap

## M0 — Project constitution ✅

- Lock the player fantasy, authority boundary and anti-toy design rules.
- Establish a typed Python domain model and deterministic tests.
- Implement one complete delayed policy chain as the architectural proof.

## M1 — Playable vertical slice ✅

The 24-month campaign contains:

- three contrasting regional associations;
- six-club top-flight simulation;
- individual first-team rosters;
- two domestic seasons and a World Cup qualifying campaign;
- opening and second-year football budgets;
- six mandatory mid-term presidential decisions;
- coach education, youth-match funding and school-football agreements;
- club bailouts, media pressure and corruption cases;
- transfer-policy choices and live transfer windows;
- monthly interactive controls and a month-24 board review.

## M2 — Professional league and club economy (near-complete)

Implemented:

- fourteen professional clubs across two levels;
- six-club National Premier League and eight-club National Championship;
- complete double round-robin scheduling at both levels;
- automatic promotion, relegation and a promotion play-off;
- licensing, solvency and wage-arrears tests before promotion;
- a fourteen-club National FA Cup in both seasons;
- an eight-club Continental Champions Cup with groups, semifinals and a final;
- central media-rights pools with equal, merit and audience shares;
- club cash, debt, wages, arrears, gate receipts and prize money;
- stadium capacity, ticket pricing, hospitality, maintenance and expansion;
- annual sponsorship contracts, bonuses and morality-clause withdrawals;
- formal administration, licence withdrawal, liquidation and phoenix clubs;
- transfer fees, wage changes and policy-driven transfer windows;
- squad registration, foreign-player limits, homegrown minima and squad caps;
- contract renewals, expiries, free agents and development loans;
- post-market registration so new loans become eligible immediately;
- schedule congestion, travel fatigue and injury risk;
- persistent owners with wealth, ambition, patience, reputation and bailout memory;
- dedicated regulatory dashboards for leagues, cups, owners, contracts, facilities and workload.

Next:

- multi-season ownership bargaining and sale of clubs;
- richer continental coefficients and travel geography;
- sponsor agents with negotiation history rather than annual formula renewal;
- municipal stadium ownership and public-private investment disputes;
- supporter boycotts and ticket-price politics.

## M3 — Player development engine (generational cycle implemented)

Implemented:

- individual players with age, position, ability, potential, fitness, morale, injuries, wages, contracts, nationality and homegrown status;
- line ratings, depth, cohesion and monthly development/decline;
- transfers, loans, contract negotiation and free agency;
- annual retirement based on age and physical decline;
- annual academy graduation of seventeen- and eighteen-year-old players;
- graduate quality linked to academy and regional development environments;
- graduates entering real wages, contracts and registration competition;
- senior national-team selection from the shared player database;
- twenty-six-player positional quotas;
- call-up scoring based on ability, fitness, morale, appearances, form, division, registration and homegrown status;
- international release causing fatigue and injury risk;
- workload rules altered by labor agreements.

Next:

- club, school and regional scouting pathways;
- U17, U20 and U23 national squads;
- formal club-versus-country release disputes;
- player agents, promises and transfer requests;
- dual-national eligibility and overseas academies;
- coaching styles affecting development curves.

## M4 — Political and organizational agents (advanced implementation)

Implemented:

- nine persistent stakeholder blocs;
- ministries, local governments, owners, players, broadcasters, sponsors and supporters;
- independent power, support, trust, patience and mobilization values;
- issue-specific preferences across integrity, grassroots, markets, fiscal policy, labor, local autonomy, national-team pressure and competitive balance;
- four national football congress agendas;
- coalition voting, legislative failure and forced passage using political capital;
- partial policy effects when legislation fails;
- all six original crisis decisions updating persistent stakeholder memory;
- measurable public promises with baselines, targets and due months;
- trust rewards for kept promises and mobilization penalties for broken promises;
- political pressure events affecting grants, execution, owners, player morale, sponsors and supporters;
- cooperation dividends from strong allies;
- sponsor revolts suspending real club revenue;
- labor bargaining changing congestion, injury and national-team release rules;
- annual coalition and governance archives;
- a political second-term review in addition to the football board review;
- three dedicated political dashboard views.

Next:

- named ministers, governors and association executives who can be appointed or removed;
- personal careers, patronage networks and institutional capture;
- multi-round negotiation before legislation rather than one final vote;
- owner threats, club relocation and forced-sale bargaining;
- formal supporter campaigns, boycotts and leadership challenges;
- sponsor relationship memory across multiple contract cycles.

## M5 — National teams and football identity (database selection implemented)

Implemented:

- a six-team World Cup qualifying group with all thirty fixtures;
- dynamic strength, expectation, results and fan-trust effects;
- coach-retention, replacement and media-management decisions;
- senior squad selection from domestic club rosters;
- injuries, appearances, form and registration affecting call-ups;
- squad quality blended with association-level coaching and preparation;
- continental, cup and political calendar decisions feeding into readiness.

Next:

- age-group national teams;
- detailed tactical identity and coaching staffs;
- selection politics and regional representation pressure;
- dual-national recruitment and overseas-player pathways;
- finals tournaments and continental championships.

## M6 — Long-run campaign (institutional-memory foundation implemented)

Implemented:

- annual political and sporting archives;
- league, cup and continental outcomes retained by season;
- stakeholder relationship memory across the full term;
- promise histories and political event timelines;
- strongest ally, opposition leader and coalition-support records;
- second-term eligibility based on sporting results, coalition strength and credibility.

Next:

- a genuinely continuous twenty-year campaign rather than repeated two-year resets;
- multi-term presidents, succession contests and institutional continuity;
- save files and deterministic reloads;
- multiple national starting scenarios;
- historical player, club, ownership and financial records;
- scenario editor and explainable long-horizon reports.
