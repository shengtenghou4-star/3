# Football Republic — Game Design Constitution

## Fantasy

The player is the fully empowered president of a national football association. The office has direct authority over football regulation, competition design, registration, licensing, national teams, development funds, integrity systems, and the association bureaucracy. It can influence—but cannot simply command—ministries, schools, police, tax authorities, broadcasters, local governments, investors, parents, and clubs.

The game asks one hard question: **can you build a football nation whose success survives your own departure?**

## Scale

The simulation runs from national policy down through:

`country -> regional association -> city -> club -> academy -> age-group squad`

A monthly turn is the default. Matches, scandals, insolvencies, elections, and major negotiations can interrupt the turn flow.

## Six coupled systems

1. **Power and governance** — mandates, political capital, appointments, enforcement, local compliance, corruption and bureaucratic capacity.
2. **Professional football** — league structure, club licensing, ownership, wage discipline, foreign-player rules, media rights and competitive balance.
3. **Youth development** — participation, coach supply, facilities, match volume, scouting, retention and transitions into professional football.
4. **National teams** — selection, coaching, tactical identity, age-group continuity, dual-national recruitment and tournament pressure.
5. **Football economy** — public funding, sponsorship, broadcasting, attendance, transfers, club solvency and reinvestment.
6. **Football society** — parents, schools, supporters, media narratives, regional inequality, legitimacy and trust.

## The president's real powers

The player can directly:

- write association and competition regulations;
- license or exclude clubs and academies;
- allocate association-controlled money;
- appoint national-team and association leadership;
- design leagues, cups, calendars and registration rules;
- set coaching, academy, stadium and financial standards;
- audit, sanction and investigate football entities;
- negotiate with external ministries and commercial partners.

The player cannot directly order a school to create a team, a city to build pitches, a bank to rescue a club, or parents to accept a risky football career. These require bargaining, incentives, legal authority, trust, or pressure.

## Non-negotiable simulation rules

- Every major output must have a visible causal path.
- Money spent is not the same as capacity created.
- Policies have implementation bottlenecks, leakage, delay and unintended responses.
- National-team results are noisy and politically powerful, but they are not the only measure of success.
- Short-term success can destroy long-term development, and long-term reform can make the first years look worse.
- Aggregate scores must be decomposable into measurable components.
- Agents can comply, resist, game the rules, form coalitions, leak information, or exploit loopholes.

## Initial scenario

The first scenario is a fictionalized large East Asian football nation beginning in 2026. Its institutions and economic constraints are recognizable, while named people and clubs remain fictional. The scenario begins with:

- weak trust after repeated governance failures;
- uneven regional development;
- fragile club finances;
- a large population but a narrow registered-player base;
- insufficient high-quality youth coaches and meaningful matches;
- intense pressure to reach the World Cup quickly;
- substantial state capacity that can be mobilized only through cross-government negotiation.

## Victory is a portfolio, not a trophy

The end-of-tenure report evaluates:

- senior and youth national-team performance;
- registered participation and retention;
- coach quantity and quality;
- match density and facility access;
- professional player production;
- league quality and financial health;
- club survival and competitive balance;
- commercial revenue and private reinvestment;
- integrity, transparency and supporter trust;
- regional inequality;
- resilience after the president leaves office.

A president may qualify for a World Cup and still leave a ruined system. Another may miss qualification but create the pipeline that produces a golden generation ten years later.
