# National-team command centre

Football Republic treats an international match as a presidential responsibility without turning the association chairman into the head coach.

## Authority boundary

The player may:

- set the association's public posture before a match;
- coordinate club release, player welfare and institutional resources;
- define whether a result enters formal performance review;
- retain, warn, review or dismiss the head coach after the match;
- accept the financial, political and sporting consequences of that decision.

The player may not:

- choose a formation or starting eleven;
- order substitutions or tactical changes;
- inspect hidden player-selection scores;
- overwrite the match engine after seeing the result.

Technical selection remains with the national-team staff. The chairman controls the environment, accountability and leadership appointments.

## Window lifecycle

1. **Preparation week** — adaptive time slows during the last seven days before a known international fixture.
2. **Presidential directive** — the chairman chooses one public and administrative posture. Time cannot cross the match boundary until this responsibility is signed.
3. **Authoritative match settlement** — the existing shared player database, squad selector, opponent, venue and match simulator produce the result.
4. **Post-match review** — score, expected goals, table position and the original directive return to the presidential office.
5. **Leadership decision** — the chairman retains, reviews, warns or dismisses the coach. Time remains frozen until the decision is recorded.

## Persistence and replay

Match directives, coach status, reviews and coaching changes are stored in executive-president save format 10.

Every sporting, financial or public-trust consequence is also written through the ordered external-action log. The parent political and football world therefore reconstructs the same consequence during deterministic save replay before the national-team office record is reattached.

Legacy executive saves remain loadable:

- format 8 opens with a reconstructed calendar and a fresh national-team office;
- format 9 preserves its adaptive calendar and opens with a fresh national-team office;
- format 10 verifies the full world, executive office, national-team command and calendar fingerprint.

## Information boundary

The command centre shows public fixtures, opponent strength, squad availability categories, results, expected goals and table position. It does not expose hidden player-selection values, coach competence formulas or deterministic future outcomes.
