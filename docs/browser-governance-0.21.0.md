# Browser governance career 0.21.0

The browser game alternates between governing the national football system and handling national-team windows.

The chairman can formulate policy across club licensing, wage security, youth development, foreign-player rules, referee governance, broadcasting, coach licensing, school football, women’s football, stadium safety, calendar protection and financial control. Every route offers competing institutional models and rollout intensity.

Policies spend budget and political capacity, change named stakeholder support, enter a delayed implementation pipeline and alter persistent football-ecology conditions. Weak conditions can generate wage, referee, stadium, broadcast and academy crises. These conditions feed back into club relations, revenue, player fatigue, the talent pool and national-team readiness.

The chairman still cannot select formations, starting elevens or substitutions.
