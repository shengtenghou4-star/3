# Coalition Elections and Government Formation

Football Republic 0.11 turns an open presidential succession into a multi-round bargaining process rather than an automatic successor roll.

## Candidate field

Every open convention begins with three routes:

- foundations: integrity, grassroots development, labor protection and competitive balance;
- balanced: fiscal discipline, regulated markets and incremental reform;
- quick results: commercial growth, local networks and immediate national-team performance.

Each route has a named candidate, a sponsoring stakeholder bloc, coalition skill, administrative skill, integrity, charisma and an issue platform.

## Weighted stakeholder vote

All nine persistent stakeholder blocs vote with their existing power values:

- national sports authority;
- finance ministry;
- education ministry;
- provincial associations and local governments;
- club owners;
- players' union;
- broadcasters;
- sponsor council;
- supporters' federation.

A bloc evaluates:

- compatibility between its issue preferences and the candidate platform;
- candidate coalition skill, charisma and integrity;
- the bloc's current trust and support;
- whether the candidate has made a targeted offer;
- prior-round momentum;
- the candidate's level of overpromising;
- the bloc's history of broken promises.

A bloc can abstain if no candidate clears its minimum credibility threshold. Winning therefore requires more than beating the other candidates: the candidate must exceed half of total weighted power, including abstentions.

## Three-stage player flow

Each round has two playable stages:

1. nominate or endorse the candidate the player wants to push;
2. choose that candidate's bargaining intensity.

Available packages:

### Clean mandate

No targeted offices, budgets or policy concessions. It protects integrity and future freedom of action but may fail to assemble a majority.

### Limited coalition agreement

Two swing blocs receive a combination of office or policy commitments. This improves the chance of winning while creating obligations due after government formation.

### Grand bargain

Four blocs receive office nominations, budget allocations or measurable policy promises. It maximizes the immediate vote but raises treasury costs, patronage risk and future breach exposure.

The first round begins with three candidates. If nobody wins, the weakest candidate is eliminated. Bargaining continues for up to three rounds. A third-round plurality can form a minority government if no candidate reaches a majority.

## Real government commitments

Election promises are not flavor text.

### Office allocations

A promised office changes the actual association cabinet. Depending on the sponsoring bloc, the coalition may control nominations for secretary-general, finance and licensing, integrity, technical or grassroots offices.

### Budget commitments

Budget agreements deduct real treasury funds and produce actual benefits such as national-team preparation, youth development, local implementation, player welfare, club finance or supporter programs.

### Policy promises

Policy commitments are given measurable baselines, targets and six-month deadlines. Examples include national-team strength, league financial health, youth environment, regional execution, solvent-club share, injury policy, club revenue, integrity and fan trust.

## Coalition stability

After installation, the government agreement calculates stability from:

- support and trust among coalition blocs;
- the scale of overpromising;
- broken or overdue commitments;
- minority-government status.

A weak agreement can trigger a coalition crisis. The president may:

- renegotiate with new spending and greater capture risk;
- end the bargain and govern as a minority;
- submit to a weighted confidence vote.

Failure in a confidence vote removes the president and starts another caretaker period and election.

## Caretaker government

Caretaker governments cannot exploit the interregnum to negotiate a new political mandate. Ordinary business is handled through a lowest-change balanced continuity option so competitions, contracts and administration continue until the election opens.

## Scheduled succession

Open succession at a normal term boundary uses the same weighted convention engine. Incumbent renewal can still avoid an open contest, but a genuine leadership vacancy is no longer filled by a scripted route choice.
