# Named Executive Implementation

Version 0.15 turns a presidential signature into the beginning of government work rather than the end of a decision screen.

The player remains one fixed football-association president. Clubs, stakeholders, cabinet officials, reporters, courts and successor governments remain autonomous.

## 1. A signed decision creates a mandate

Every ordinary major presidential decision creates an implementation mandate containing:

- the original presidential decision and selected option;
- a list of offices whose responsibilities appear relevant;
- the named office-holder eventually selected by the chairman;
- the chairman's instruction style;
- a review deadline;
- a public implementation status;
- the督查室's current explanation of progress or delay.

A mandate begins without an owner. The chairman must name one current senior official. If no one is assigned by the next month, departments wait for one another, political capital falls and the failure remains in the office record.

Resignation and other decisions that immediately end the player's authority do not create a mandate for the former chairman.

## 2. Named officials are causal

The chosen official is not a cosmetic card or a flat percentage modifier. Delivery responds to:

- competence;
- loyalty to the current chairman;
- personal integrity;
- political and institutional networks;
- scandal exposure;
- fit between the mandate and the official's office;
- clarity and flexibility of the chairman's instructions;
- the number of other active mandates assigned to the same office;
- accumulated grievance inside the cabinet;
- a small deterministic uncertainty term.

These exact calculations are hidden from the player. The chairman observes named responsibility, deadlines, departmental language, visible progress, missed milestones and eventual results.

An official may:

- deliver the mandate substantially as signed;
- complete only part of it;
- delay it;
- quietly narrow the original scope;
- fail to produce a verifiable result.

The outcome changes stakeholder trust, political capital and the named official's loyalty, network position or scandal exposure.

## 3. The chairman chooses an instruction style

### Strict compliance

The chairman gives a narrow, highly legible instruction. It reduces room for quiet reinterpretation but can make coalition management and adaptation harder.

### Outcome delegation

The chairman specifies the required result and lets the department choose the method. It can move faster, but gives the office-holder more room to redefine what counts as success.

### Negotiated implementation

The chairman authorizes the official to negotiate while delivering. It can reduce outside resistance, but is slower and creates more opportunities for the original policy to be narrowed.

There is no universally correct style. Its value depends on the official, mandate, coalition and surrounding crisis.

## 4. Competing reports

The same mandate can generate reports from three different offices:

- the assigned implementation office;
- another office with professional jurisdiction;
- a political, financial or procedural office.

Each report exposes:

- its source and named author;
- a recommendation;
- the evidence or responsibility behind the recommendation;
- a likely blind spot;
- a broad confidence assessment;
- urgency.

The game does not label one report as the correct answer. Conflicts can reflect legitimate professional differences, incomplete evidence, departmental self-protection or actual implementation distortion.

Hidden progress, delivery-quality and distortion values are never printed in the player interface.

## 5. Live press conferences

The chairman can hold a press conference with up to three rounds of questioning.

A reporter's next question reacts to the previous answer:

- rule-first answers are challenged on the human and competitive cost of strict rules;
- promises to support the sector are challenged on funding and eligibility;
- admitted uncertainty is challenged on why the chairman spoke before facts were complete;
- no-comment answers are challenged as evidence that the private situation may be worse.

Changing directly from strict rule enforcement to broad sector support can create an immediate credibility contradiction. Repeated refusal to answer can make the chairman's silence the story.

Every exact quotation also enters the existing long-term public-statement system, so a future policy reversal can revive an old press-conference answer.

## 6. Persistence and replay

Executive-president saves use format version 8.

The save contains:

- all implementation mandates;
- named assignments and instruction styles;
- deadlines, public status and outcomes;
- competing departmental reports;
- full press-conference question-and-answer sequences;
- the underlying causal-office and political-world history.

Loading first reconstructs and verifies the fixed-player causal history, then restores the executive layer and checks a combined fingerprint. A corrupted or reordered save is rejected.

## 7. Player authority boundary

The player may assign and supervise implementation only while the original chairman remains in office.

After resignation, removal, electoral defeat or constitutional rotation:

- implementation authority is locked;
- press-conference authority is locked;
- successor government choices are autonomous;
- the player may only observe the continuing football world.

This system exists to make the player's presidency more demanding, not to let the player control every official or institution.
